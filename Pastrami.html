Homemade Deli-Style Pastrami Guide

A step-by-step guide to curing, smoking, and steaming a UK supermarket rolled brisket into authentic, tender pastrami using a Ninja Woodfire and a Ninja Foodi.

Meat Specification: This guide is tailored for a standard 1.158 kg rolled Scotch Beef brisket joint. The curing math and timings are calculated specifically for this weight and thickness.

The Master Method

Phase 1: The Equilibrium Cure

  1. Prep the Meat: Snip the strings and completely unroll the brisket joint. Pat it completely dry with kitchen paper. Trim off any loose, overly ragged bits of meat if necessary.
  2. Apply the Cure: Weigh out exactly 58g of the Angus & Oink cure mix. Rub the mix evenly over every single surface, flap, and fold of the unrolled brisket. Use all of the measured cure.
  3. Seal the Bag: Place the seasoned brisket into a heavy-duty zip-lock bag. Use the water displacement method to force out the air: lower the open bag into a sink filled with water up to the zip line, then seal it shut.
  4. The Fridge Cure (4–5 Days Minimum): Place the bag flat in the fridge. Crucial: Turn the bag over once every 24 hours. A liquid brine will form as salt draws out moisture; flipping ensures an even cure. Leave it in the cure for 4 to 5 days minimum (an extra day or two is perfectly fine and safe).

Phase 2: Rinsing & Developing the Pellicle

  1. The Wash: Remove the brisket from the bag. Rinse it thoroughly under cold running tap water to remove all excess surface salt and cure crystals. Pat completely dry with kitchen paper.
  2. Air Drying (24–48 Hours): Place the brisket uncovered on a stainless steel wire cooling rack set inside a baking tray. Place it back in the fridge. This dry, moving air will form a tacky, shiny skin called a pellicle, which helps the smoke and rub stick perfectly.

Phase 3: The Smoke (Ninja Woodfire)

  1. The Rub: Apply a very thin layer of American mustard or a splash of water to the meat to act as a binder. Coat the brisket heavily with the final pastrami spice rub, pressing it firmly into the tacky pellicle layer.
  2. Set Up the Smoker: Fill the pellet hopper with strong wood pellets (Hickory or Oak). Set the Ninja Woodfire to SMOKE at a temperature between 105°C and 110°C.
  3. Smoke Phase: Place the brisket directly on the grill grate. Smoke until the internal temperature of the meat reaches 71°C (160°F) and the dark spice crust is firmly set. This usually takes around 2 to 3 hours.

Phase 4: The Authentic Deli Steam (Ninja Foodi)

  1. Set Up the Foodi: Pour approximately 500ml of boiling water into the bottom of the main Ninja Foodi pot. Place the reversible rack or crisp basket inside, ensuring the meat will sit elevated completely clear of the water.
  2. Transfer the Meat: Move the smoked brisket straight from the Woodfire and place it uncovered onto the Foodi rack.
  3. Steam Setting: Close the Foodi lid. Ensure the valve is set to **VENT** (do not pressure cook). Select the STEAM function (at 100°C if your model allows adjustable temperatures).
  4. Cook to Tenderness: Steam for 1.5 to 2 hours. The pastrami is fully finished when the internal temperature hits 95°C to 98°C (203°F to 208°F) and an internal temperature probe slides effortlessly into the center of the meat like soft butter.

Serving

Remove the pastrami from the steamer and let it rest on a clean cutting board for 20 minutes. Always carve the pastrami across the grain as thinly as possible for the ultimate tender bite.
