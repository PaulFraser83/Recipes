Texas-Style BBQ Beef Brisket Guide (Uncured)

A step-by-step master method for cooking a fresh, uncured UK supermarket rolled brisket into a beautifully tender, smoky BBQ brisket using the Ninja Woodfire Grill.

No Curing Required: This method completely skips the multi-day curing stage. It is optimized to transform a standard ~1.1kg fresh brisket joint into a juicy BBQ style brisket using the "Texas Wrap" technique directly on the grill.

Equipment & Ingredients

Item Specification / Quantity
Beef Brisket ~1.1kg fresh rolled brisket joint (completely untied)
BBQ Rub Angus & Oink Pastrami Rub, a dedicated BBQ beef rub, or equal parts coarse sea salt, black pepper, and garlic powder.
Binder A small amount of American mustard or olive oil (to help the rub stick)
Hardware Ninja Woodfire Grill, strong wood pellets (Hickory or Oak), heavy-duty aluminum foil, and an instant-read meat probe.

The Master Method

Phase 1: Prep & Seasoning

  1. Unroll the Joint: Snip the butcher's strings and completely unroll the brisket joint. Lay it as flat as possible. Pat the entire surface completely dry with kitchen paper.
  2. Apply the Binder: Slather a very thin layer of American mustard or olive oil over all sides of the beef. This won't impact the final taste; it simply acts as a glue for your spices.
  3. Coat with Rub: Generously shake your BBQ rub over the entire brisket. Press the spices firmly onto the meat with your hands to build a heavy, even layer. Make sure to get into any folds or uneven edges.

Phase 2: The Smoke Phase

  1. Fire up the Grill: Fill the pellet hopper of your Ninja Woodfire with your chosen wood pellets. Set the unit to the SMOKE function and set the temperature to 105°C to 110°C (220°F to 230°F).
  2. The Smoke Cook: Place the seasoned, unrolled brisket directly onto the cold grill grate. Close the lid and let it smoke undisturbed.
  3. Target Bark & Temp: Leave it on the smoker until the internal temperature of the thickest part of the meat reaches roughly 71°C to 74°C (160°F to 165°F). At this point, the outside crust (the bark) should look dark, mahogany-colored, and firmly set. This usually takes about 2 to 3 hours.

Phase 3: The Texas Wrap

  1. Wrap Tightly: Remove the brisket from the grill and place it onto a large sheet of heavy-duty aluminum foil. Wrap the meat incredibly tightly in a double layer of foil, creating a sealed parcel that traps all the steam and escaping juices. *(Optional: Add 1-2 tablespoons of beef broth or Worcestershire sauce into the bottom of the foil parcel just before sealing).*
  2. The Braising Finish: Place the fully wrapped foil parcel back onto the Ninja Woodfire grill grate.
  3. Adjust Settings: Turn the Woodfire to the BAKE or ROAST setting at a temperature of 115°C to 120°C (240°F to 250°F).
  4. Check for Doneness: Cook until the internal temperature of the brisket reaches between 95°C to 98°C (203°F to 208°F). Use an instant-read probe to test the texture—it should slide through the foil and into the center of the meat with absolutely zero resistance, feeling just like soft butter.

Phase 4: The Crucial Rest

  1. Hold the Heat: Once perfectly tender, remove the wrapped brisket from the grill. Do not open the foil.
  2. The Towel Trick: Wrap the entire foil parcel inside an old bath towel and place it inside a clean, empty plastic coolbox or your turned-off, cold kitchen oven.
  3. Rest Duration: Let the meat rest for 1 to 2 hours. This allows the hot muscle fibers to relax and reabsorb all the liquid back into the beef, ensuring your brisket stays incredibly juicy when sliced.

Serving

Unwrap the rested brisket carefully on a rimmed cutting board to catch any remaining juices. Identify the direction of the meat fibers (the grain) and carve the brisket across the grain into slices roughly the thickness of a pound coin.
